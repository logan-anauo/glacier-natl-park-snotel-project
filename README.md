<h1>Glacier National Park SNOTEL Stations</h1>

<div align="justify">
Snow Telemetry (SNOTEL) is a system of automated sensor stations strategically placed in the western United States that collect hourly data pertaining to weather and climate. These stations are administered by the U.S. Department of Agriculture - Natural Resources Conservation Service in their effort to monitor water supply, predict flood events, and plan for droughts, among other things. A standard SNOTEL station is outfitted with a suit of instruments to measure and log snow depth, snow-water equivalent (SWE), precipitation, air temperature, soil moisture, and soil temperature.
